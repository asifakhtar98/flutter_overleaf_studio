\section{Introduction}

Einstein's theory of special relativity \cite{einstein1905} introduced the famous
mass--energy equivalence relation $E = mc^2$.

Newton's laws of motion \cite{newton1687} form the foundation of classical mechanics.
The inverse-square law of gravitation was later unified with electromagnetism
through Maxwell's equations \cite{maxwell1865}.

Modern quantum mechanics builds upon these foundations, with the Schr\"odinger
equation \cite{schrodinger1926} providing the wave-mechanical formulation.
